Cramps

What are Cramps?

Cramps are caused because the muscle’s of a female’s uterus are contracting to help push out the blood through the vagina during her period. These contractions of the uterus are triggered by chemicals called prostaglandins in the lining of the uterus. The amount of these chemicals affect the amount of pain one feels. Girls who have relatives that have regular cramps during periods might experience cramps as well

Pre- Menstrual Cycle (PMS)

Cramps and other symptoms also occur before the menstruation cycle Factors involved in PMS

    Low levels of vitamins and mineral
    Eating a lot of salty foods, which may cause you to retain fluid
    Drinking alcohol and caffeine, which may alter your mood and energy level

Types of Cramps

There are two types of dysmenorrhea

Primary dysmenorrhea

    This is the type of cramps are what most girls experience
    Pain is usually felt in the lower belly/lower back
    Can start 1-2 days before your period and last for 2-4 days

Secondary dysmenorrhea

    endometriosis or pelvic inflammatory disease cause these cramps to occur
    These type of camps usually come early in the menstrual cycle and last longer than primary dysmenorrhea
