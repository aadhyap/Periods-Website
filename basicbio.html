The Female Reproductive System

The Parts:

Fallopian Tube: The tube an egg travels down to get to the uterus. If an egg is to be fertilized, this is where the sperm will combine with the egg and form a zygote

Uterus: Also known as the womb, this is where a fetilized egg develops into a fetus during pregnancy.

Ovary: The small glands on opposite sides of the uterus which contains eggs before they are released into the Fallopian Tubes

Endometrium: The inner lining of the uterus which thickens during the mentsrual cycle in order to prepare to support a fetilized egg's growth into a fetus

Cervix: The tissue that connects the vagina and the uterus

Vagina: The tube that connects the cervix to the outside of the body. This is how a baby exits the mother during childbirth and how blood exits during menstration

Vulva: The name of all the external female genitalia in the pubic region, including the mons pubis, the labia majora, the labia minora, and the clotoris

The Stages of the Menstrual Cycle

Stage 1: The Period

The uterus sheds its lining. This is the beginning of the menstrual cycle. During this time, a woman bleeds about 30-40 mls (about 5 to 10 teaspoons of blood) and experiences cramps. This stage usually lasts from two to seven days with the beginning days having the heaviest bleeding and most painful cramps. A period is considered heavy if the woman bleeds over 60 mls. About 90% of women bleed less than 80 mls during their period.

Stage 2: Preparing the next egg

A new egg gets prepared to be released from the ovary and the uterus lining beings to thicken in preparation for a fertilized egg to attach grow into a fetus. The thickening of the uterus occurs whether or not the egg will be fertilized later.

Stage 3: Ovulation

The egg is released from the ovary in a process called ovulation. During this time some women experience one sided cramps known as middleschmertz, the German word for “middle pain”.

Stage 4: Traveling down the Fallopian Tube

The egg continues down the fallopian tube on its way to the uterus which is continually thickening.

Stage 5: Enter the uterus

The egg reaches the uterus. If the egg is fertilized it will attach to the uterus wall and begin development. If not, the cycle begins again at stage one: menstruation.
